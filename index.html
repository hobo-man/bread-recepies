Bannock

  • Makes eight
  • Prep and cook: one hour plus one hour to rise

ingredients

  1. 3 1/4 cups of bread flower
  2. 1/3 cup of cornmeal
  3. 1 1/2 teaspoons of yeast
  4. 1 teaspoon of salt
  5. 1 1/2 cups of warm water
  6. 1/3 cup of olive oil and some more for brushing
  7. sea salt flakes to serve

  1. Place the flower, cornmeal, yeast, and salt in a bowl and mix together. add the measured water and 3 tablespoons of the oil and mix togeather to form a dough.
  2. knead the dough in an electric mixer for 5 minutes, or by and on a lightly flowered surface for 10 minutes, until the dough is soft and springy. place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap and leave in a warm plsce to rise for about an hour until the mixture doubled it's size.
  3. tip the dough out onto a lightly flowered surface, then punch it down and knead it a few times until the air is out of the mixture. divide the dough into eight equil pieces and keep covered with a lightly oiled sheet of plastic wrap. roll out the dough until pieces are one-quarter inch thick.
  4. Heat a pan until smoking hot(preferably not a nonstick for safty reasons) brush over the Bannock wih a little oil, then cook, in batches, for 3 to 5 minutes on each side until lightly charred and cooked through. Tear into large chuncks and serve warm, or just eat it like a normal flat breadsprikle with some sea salt flakes if desired

oatmeal

ingredients

  1. 1/2 cup of oatmeal
  2. 1 cup of water or milk

  • one cup of water and one half cup of oats per serving
  1. boil water (or milk) in a pot.
  2. pour dry oats into the boiling water and reduce to medium heat
  3. stir occasionally at least 5 minutes or until desired thickness
  4. serve with toppings such as
    • penut butter
    • chopped nuts
    • brown sugar
    • coconut
    • butter
